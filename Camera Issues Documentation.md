# Camera Issues Documentation

## 1. Identify Camera Issues

Below is a list of potential camera problems:

*   **Camera blocked:** This issue occurs when there is a physical obstruction preventing the camera from capturing images or video correctly. The obstruction could be dust, a foreign object, or even part of the camera's surroundings.

*   **Camera disconnected:** This issue indicates a loss of connection between the camera and the system it uses. This could be due to cable problems, network issues, connection settings, or even a camera malfunction.

## 2. Scenario Documentation

To further clarify these issues, we can consider the following scenarios:

### Scenario 1: Camera Blocked

**Description:** An object is placed in front of the camera lens, completely or partially obstructing the view.

**Potential Causes:**
*   Accumulation of dirt or dust on the lens.
*   A physical object (such as a piece of paper, cloth, or even a hand) in front of the camera.
*   A change in the camera's environment leading to obstruction (e.g., plant growth).

**Impact:**
*   Blurred or unclear image/video.
*   Inability to monitor the target area.
*   Potential for false alarms if the system relies on image analysis.

### Scenario 2: Camera Disconnected

**Description:** The camera loses connection to the network or central system, preventing data transmission.

**Potential Causes:**
*   Disconnected network or power cable.
*   Faulty network port or camera's network card.
*   Network configuration issues (e.g., incorrect IP address).
*   Router or switch malfunction.
*   Camera driver or firmware issues.

**Impact:**
*   No video or image feed available from the camera.
*   Inability to control the camera remotely.
*   Loss of critical data that the camera was supposed to record.
*   Potential failure to detect important security or operational events.
